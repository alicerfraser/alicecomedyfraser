Alice Fraser, who left Allens to do comedy full-time, say [s her]... corporate experience has been invaluable. "I find it much easier to do comedy now I've done law," Fraser says. "You know what a work ethic looks like and I know exactly how much my time is worth."

"It is an unsettling thing to interview comedians who speak fluent legalese, particularly when they refer to the benefits of being "a little bit more client focused" than their peers. "A lot of comedians are very focused on themselves, but once you're in the law environment, you become a product and you are being sold," Fraser says.

"Part of that is figuring out what you offer the client. So for comedy, it's not 'how do I make myself a star' but 'what do I bring to the table that the audience might want'?"
